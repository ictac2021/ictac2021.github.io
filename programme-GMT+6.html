LIST OF ACCEPTED PAPERS

The scientific programme consists of four keynote talks and the following accepted papers:

  • Card-based Cryptographic Protocols with a Standard Deck of Cards Using Private Operations
    Yoshifumi Manabe and Hibiki Ono
  • Learning Probabilistic Automata using Residuals
    Wenjing Chu, Shuo Chen and Marcello Bonsangue
  • ComplexityParser: an automatic tool for certifying poly-time complexity of Java programs
    Emmanuel Hainry, Emmanuel Jeandel, Romain Péchoux and Olivier Zeyen
  • On the Correctness Problem for Serializability
    Jürgen König and Heike Wehrheim
  • A Complete Semantics of K and Its Translation to Isabelle
    Liyi Li and Elsa Gunter
  • Assessing Security of Crypto-Currencies with Attack-Defense Trees: Proof of Concept and Future Directions
    Julia Eisentraut, Stephan Holzer, Katharina Klioba, Jan Kretinsky, Lukas Pin and Alexander Wagner
  • A divide & conquer approach to conditional stable model checking
    Yati Phyo, Canh Do Minh and Kazuhiro Ogata
  • A Set Automaton to Locate All Pattern Matches in a Term
    Rick Erkens and Jan Friso Groote
  • Normalising Lustre Preserves Security
    Sanjiva Prasad and Rathnakar Madhukar Yerraguntla
  • Certifying Choreography Compilation
    Luís Cruz-Filipe, Fabrizio Montesi and Marco Peressotti
  • A New Connective in Natural Deduction, and its Application to Quantum Computing
    Alejandro Díaz-Caro and Gilles Dowek
  • Accelerating SpMV Multiplication in Probabilistic Model Checkers using GPUs
    Muhammad Hannan Khan, Osman Hasan and Shahid Khan
  • AlCons: Deductive Synthesis of Sorting Algorithms in Theorema
    Isabela Dramnesc and Tudor Jebelean
  • A Mechanically Verified Theory of Contracts
    Stéphane Kastenbaum, Benoît Boyer and Jean-Pierre Talpin
  • A Calculus for Attribute-based Memory Updates
    Michele Pasqua and Marino Miculan
  • Some Aspects of the Database Resilience
    Luis Bustamante and Ana Teresa Martins
  • A Proof Method for Local Sufficient Completeness of Term Rewriting Systems
    Tomoki Shiraishi, Kentaro Kikuchi and Takahito Aoto
  • Reactive Synthesis from Visibly Register Pushdown Automata
    Ryoma Senda, Yoshiaki Takata and Hiroyuki Seki
  • Compositional Analysis of Protocol Equivalence in the Applied pi-calculus using Quasi-Open Bisimilarity
    Ross Horne, Sjouke Mauw and Semen Yurkov
  • An Incentive Mechanism for Trading Personal Data in Data Market
    Sayan Biswas, Kangsoo Jung and Catuscia Palamidessi
